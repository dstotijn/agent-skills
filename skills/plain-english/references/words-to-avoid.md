# Words to avoid

Swap these common bureaucratic words for the plainer alternative in brackets. Treat them as defaults, not bans: keep the original if the plainer word would change the meaning or misfit the reader.

- additional (extra)
- advise (tell)
- applicant (you)
- commence (start)
- complete (fill in)
- comply with (keep to)
- consequently (so)
- ensure (make sure)
- forward (send)
- in accordance with (under, keeping to)
- in excess of (more than)
- in respect of (for)
- in the event of (if)
- on receipt (when we/you get)
- on request (if you ask)
- particulars (details)
- per annum (a year)
- persons (people)
- prior to (before)
- purchase (buy)
- regarding (about)
- should you wish (if you want)
- terminate (end)
- whilst (while)

This is a short list. When a word feels stiff or official but is not here, apply the same test: is there an everyday word the reader would use in speech?
